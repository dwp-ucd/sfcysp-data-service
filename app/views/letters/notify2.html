Dear Research Participant,

You can get your State Pension on 6 September 2027
Your estimate up to 5 April 2022 is
£67.49 a week

Your forecast:

• is not a guarantee and is based on the current law
• does not include any increase due to inflation.

State Pension is usually paid every 4 weeks in arrears.

You need to contribute National Insurance for another 4 years to reach your forecast.

You have 7 years on your National Insurance record up to 5 April 2022. You need at least
10 years on your record to get any State Pension.

When you reach State Pension age on 6 September 2027, you no longer pay National
Insurance contributions.

This date may be different if you are self-employed. To find out more go to
www.gov.uk/tax-national-insurance-after-state-pension-age/stopping-paying-
national-insurance

Your forecast may be different if there are any changes to your National Insurance
information. There is more about this in the leaflet we have sent you with this letter.
