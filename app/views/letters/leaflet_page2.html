Pension sharing on divorce

When a marriage or civil partnership ends, the courts could make a pension sharing order. This is an
amount which is added to or taken away from your State Pension.

For more information on your pension sharing order call 0800 731 0175 or textphone 0800 731 0176.

Paying tax on your State Pension

You may have to pay tax on your State Pension, depending on your income and the tax rate that
applies to you.

For more information, go to www.gov.uk/tax-on-pension

If you have worked or lived abroad

Your State Pension forecast is based on your UK National Insurance record. You may also get a
State Pension from the country you worked or lived in. If you meet the eligibility criteria, you will have
to claim your pension from that country.

For more information, go to www.gov.uk/new-state-pension/living-and-working-overseas

How to claim your State Pension

You will not get your State Pension automatically – you have to claim it. If you live in the UK, you should get a letter up to 2 months before you reach State Pension age telling you what to do. If you live abroad, go to www.gov.uk/state-pension-if-you-retire-abroad State Pension if you retire abroad If you live outside of the UK, on or around April of each year (when the payments usually increase),
your payments will only increase if you live in:

• a European Economic Area (EEA) country
• Gibraltar
• Switzerland, or
• a country that has a social security reciprocal agreement with the UK (this does not include
Canada or New Zealand).

For more information, including which countries are in the EEA and have a reciprocal agreement with
the UK, go to www.gov.uk/state-pension-if-you-retire-abroad/rates-of-state-pension
