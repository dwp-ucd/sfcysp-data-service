About your State Pension



Your State Pension forecast is provided for your information only. When planning for your retirement,
you should consider seeking professional advice.

The Money Helper service offers free independent advice. To find out more go to
www.moneyhelper.org.uk or call 0800 138 7777.

Your State Pension forecast letter is based on your National Insurance record at the time you
contacted us.

To check your State Pension forecast go to www.gov.uk/check-state-pension

Your State Pension age

This is the earliest date you can get your State Pension. The State Pension age may change in the
future.

Deferring your State Pension

You can claim your State Pension even if you carry on working. You can also delay claiming, which
means you may get extra State Pension when you do claim it.

To find out more, go to www.gov.uk/deferring-state-pension

Your State Pension forecast may change

  • you may not reach your State Pension forecast if you do not add any more years to your
    National Insurance record
  • your State Pension forecast is based on the current State Pension rate. This usually goes up
    every year
  • your State Pension forecast is based on your own National Insurance record. In some cases,
    • you may be able to inherit some State Pension from your husband, wife or civil partner.

For more information about inheriting State Pension, go to www.gov.uk/new-state-
pension/inheriting-or-increasing-state-pension-from-a-spouse-or-civil-partner

How to get more State Pension

You can contribute to your State Pension by:

  • paying enough National Insurance while working
  • being treated as paying National Insurance contributions
  • getting National Insurance credits if you are unable to work because of unemployment, illness,
    disability or caring for someone
  • paying voluntary National Insurance contributions.
